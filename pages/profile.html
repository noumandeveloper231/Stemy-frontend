FAQ

Questions, answered

Everything you need to know before you upload your first track.

What's the difference between regular mastering and stem mastering?
Regular (two-track) mastering polishes a finished stereo mixdown — one file in, one mastered file out. It fixes overall tone and loudness but can't touch individual elements. This is what the Basic plan does, and it's all most artists ever need.

Stem mastering lets you upload separate groups (drums, bass, vocals, instruments, FX) so the master can treat each one independently — tighten the low end, lift the vocal, widen the drums — then glue everything together. It's how major-label records get that finished, three-dimensional sound. Stem mastering is Pro-only, accessed through the full SSL-style mixing console.
How does Stemy compare to eMastered and LANDR?
eMastered is $19.99/month and LANDR Pro is $49/month — both auto-mastering only. Stemy's Basic plan is $9.99/month for unlimited mastering, and Pro is $24.99/month with full stem mastering and an SSL-style mixing console. You get tighter control and lower cost, backed by a platinum producer rather than a black-box algorithm.
How long does it take to get my master back?
Most masters are ready in under 90 seconds. Upload your file, pick a style, and the finished master lands in your dashboard before your coffee's done. Pro users get priority processing that jumps the queue at peak times, so you're never waiting. You'll also get an email the moment it's ready to download.
What file formats can I upload and download?
Upload: WAV, AIFF, or FLAC at 16-bit or 24-bit, any sample rate from 44.1 kHz up. MP3 uploads work too but lossless sources always sound better.

Download: WAV (CD-ready and streaming-ready versions) plus a 320 kbps MP3 for quick sharing. Pro includes platform-specific targets (Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube, Tidal, SoundCloud).
How should I prep my stems before uploading?
Export each stem with all its processing baked in (EQ, compression, effects), keep peaks around −6 dBFS so there's headroom to work with, and make sure every stem starts at bar one — same length, same start — so they line up when dropped in. No need to normalize. A short guide with templates for Logic, Ableton, FL Studio, and Pro Tools is in your dashboard after signup.
Can I cancel anytime?
Yes — cancel from your account settings in two clicks. No fees, no phone calls, no "retention specialist." Monthly plans end at your next billing date; yearly plans run until the end of the term. Anything you've already mastered is yours to keep forever.
Do you own my music after I upload it?
No. You own 100% of your music. Uploading gives us permission to process it for mastering and nothing else. We don't claim rights, we don't use it for training, and we don't share it with third parties. Files are encrypted in transit and at rest, and you can delete them from your dashboard whenever you want.
Who actually masters my track?
Stemy is built around the ears and gear chain of Choo The Specialist — a platinum-certified producer with 30+ years in the game. The processing chain mirrors how Choo masters records in the studio, so you're not getting a generic AI preset, you're getting a signature sound. Pro users can optionally add a "Mastered by Choo" credit.
What if I'm not happy with the master?
You get free revisions — tell us what you want changed (more bottom, less bright, louder for streaming, whatever) and we'll re-run it. If it's still not hitting, we'll refund your track. We'd rather you sound great than stay subscribed out of frustration.
Still have a question? Email us and we usually get back within a few hours.
